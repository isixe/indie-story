## Foreword

I picked up my phone and glanced at the calendar - December was already half over, which meant 2024 was about to end. As usual, it was time for bloggers to write their annual year-end summaries. Like every year, I had no idea where to start. But unlike previous years, I did not hesitate at all about whether to write one.

First, **2024 was a truly special year for me - it was my first year as a full-time freelancer and indie developer. I left the workforce, stopped going to an office, supported myself, woke up naturally every day, and still made over 10,000 RMB a month. This will definitely be a major turning point in my career.**

Second, both my personal blog (blog.axiaoxin.com) and my WeChat Official Account needed content - this was a perfect opportunity.

Third, I occasionally receive private messages from readers asking how I have been doing.

So my attitude became firm: spending the time on this was worth it. **This article aims to be honest and grounded, without too much "success story" packaging. It is just a regular person sharing the confusion, anxiety, growth, and gains during the transition.**

Before writing this, I specifically looked up the definition of a year-end summary:

> A year-end summary is a type of applied writing where people **review** and **analyze** their work and study over the past year, identify experiences and lessons, derive regular patterns, and guide future work and practice. It covers an overview of the year, achievements, experiences, lessons, and future directions.
>
> - Baidu Baike

2024 also marks ten years since I graduated from university. So let me start with how I ended up on the path to full-time freelancing, then talk about the 2024 overview, achievements and shortcomings, lessons learned, and my outlook for 2025. The content is messy and long (over 20,000 Chinese characters) - just some observations and thoughts from the current moment, not necessarily correct, for reference only.

## Ten Years

Although the biggest turning point this year was becoming a full-time freelancer and living the dream life of not commuting and waking up naturally, this year also marks the tenth anniversary of my university graduation. Counting internships and overtime, I have been working for over a decade. Life does not have many decades, so before I begin, I want to reflect on the youth that has passed. Ten years on, friends have become old friends, and we have mostly lost touch - everyone has their own concerns. Especially for someone like me, adrift away from home, there are far fewer topics to discuss. But after all these years, I am used to it. I have always been a person who is comfortable with solitude.

### First Steps into Programming

My connection with programming began in my senior year of high school. Somehow, I downloaded an app called "PY Programming Easy" on my Nokia S60 smartphone - it was like an IDE (Integrated Development Environment) on a phone. Back then, I did not really understand it. It had tutorials and runnable code. When I pressed the button and the code executed, a pop-up appeared on my phone. The feeling was truly - I am not exaggerating - "shocking." So I became obsessed with writing Nokia phone apps, right on the phone itself. I wrote a program called "Phone QQ Chat Assistant" and distributed it as an attachment on Symbian phone forums. Its main feature was simulating key presses to perform automated operations on mobile QQ - like automatically copying emoji expressions and sending them to friends. There was also an annoying feature that constantly toggled between "Online" and "Invisible" status. Back then, many people used QQ on their computers, and every login/logout made a sound - so a flurry of automated operations would spam people. Developing phone software, on a phone - the temptation was too great for someone about to take the college entrance exam. I learned by searching for tutorials others had written online. Nowadays, people would sell those tutorials as knowledge products. I wrote all the code on a T9 keypad phone - thinking back, that was pretty hardcore. Online tutorials were scarce back then, and I longed to study this systematically at university. Although our high school computer classes taught VB programming, everyone just used that time to browse the web. I had no feeling for programming at all. So **interest really is the best teacher**. Even now, looking back at that experience, I still feel excited. I chose Software Engineering as my university major, and from there I embarked on my career as a software engineer (commonly known as a programmer). **My personal opinion: the computer industry was a good choice for ordinary people ten years ago. In the future, despite the intense competition, I still think it will remain a good industry.**

Ten years ago, I was young, energetic, and full of hope for the future. I started looking for jobs in my junior year, studying technologies relevant to Python positions that interested me. I remember distinctly completing a written test for Knownsec in my junior year - they wanted a crawler written in Python. I attended an on-site interview in Chengdu but did not pass - they wanted a full-time commitment, and I still had classes. Back then, all I thought about was getting a job early, earning my own money. I never considered starting a business, let alone leaving Sichuan. At university, besides various programming language course projects, I enjoyed tinkering and building small tools in my free time - like a CET-4/6 score checker that did not require a registration number, and an online quiz platform for the annual Geek Challenge by the Shamrock Tech Group. My coding experience and product implementation skills were decent, I would say. I taught myself Python throughout university because I loved it - I borrowed and read every Python book in the library.

![University Web development primer book](/content/axiaoxin-2024-summary/django-book.png)

This was the Django Web Development Guide I bought on Amazon back then. Following this tutorial, I built my own blog website in Python, which I used until 2022. Having this blog helped me present myself better and opened up more job opportunities. As work got busy, I gradually stopped maintaining the blog and rarely updated it. In 2023, for the sake of simple writing and deployment, I switched to a static site.

### Entering the Workplace

At university, we learned "mainstream" languages like C, C++, and Java. Python-related positions were scarce, especially at well-known domestic companies like Baidu, Alibaba, and Tencent - they basically did not hire for Python. So I started to feel confused: should I find a job related to a programming language I was interested in, or should I master languages I did not like and try to get into a big company? Back then, I was not just a tech enthusiast - I was also a narrow-minded "youngster" obsessed with which programming language to use. **No matter what programming language, it is just a tool to achieve specific goals.** Since my university was not prestigious, many recruitment events did not come to our campus. During graduation season, I attended job fairs at neighboring Sichuan University, submitted some resumes, attended some interviews - but nothing came of them. Back then, I did not feel any anxiety at all. Unlike now, where I get anxious over everything. I really miss that. Eventually, I went to Codoon in Chengdu for a Python internship. By chance, I was called to work in Shanghai by "mrluanma," who was just an online acquaintance at the time. Meeting him was fate - Weibo was popular in college, and when I was learning Python, I liked to tweet about problems I encountered. We connected on Weibo. I did not overthink it - I jumped on a green train and rode 40+ hours from Chengdu to Shanghai. That was around October 2013. There probably was not a direct high-speed rail yet, and I had never flown before.

In Shanghai, I witnessed the prosperity of a big city and developed some new ideas: I wanted to work a few years in each of China's major cities - Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou - using each work city as a hub to travel around the surrounding areas. In a few years, I could work and travel across the entire eastern part of China at the same time. Then I would return to Sichuan, use it as a hub to explore the west, and effortlessly combine work and travel across all of China.

**Great in theory, harsh in reality.** I stayed in Shanghai for 3 years, and I had pretty much explored Shanghai and its surrounding cities. After seeing what Beijing workers go through online, I felt their happiness index was not high. So I switched from Ele.me in Shanghai to Tencent in Shenzhen, Guangdong. Before the pandemic hit, I started thinking about going back to Sichuan. There was a period where I lost sight of my life goals, felt no hope for anything, did not know the meaning of life - lost and adrift. But as the pandemic unfolded and work got busy, that plan was shelved. Even now, I occasionally feel this way - that life is meaningless. But the difference is, before it was more negative; now it is more about letting go. I can say without reservation: **I love life, but I am pessimistic about this world.** I am not a materialistic person. I do not crave a mansion or luxury cars - I do not see them as necessities. My only real necessity is the **sense of security** I crave deep inside.

Until 2020, in this seemingly familiar yet unfamiliar cold city, I found love that felt far away yet right in front of me. It was truly fate. In 2022, we registered our marriage in Nanshan District, Shenzhen - a major life milestone accomplished. And just like that, it was 2024. I have been in Shenzhen for 8 years now. The two vertical lines on my forehead have deepened, I have gotten greasier and fatter, my youth is gone but my acne is not... Ten years ago, you could at least see my facial contours. Now I have rounded into a ball :(

![Ten years ago](/content/axiaoxin-2024-summary/ten-years-ago.png)

Looking back at that plan now, it is nearly impossible to complete. Going to work in Beijing is almost out of the question. These 10 years have shown me how much in life is beyond our control. I have felt how small individuals are. I have been through dark, difficult lows and had good times too. But luckily, no major disasters - I have been fortunate. Life's trajectory keeps shifting with the people and events you encounter, constantly pushing forward in unpredictable ways. Maybe that is what they call "fate."

### Confusion and Struggle: The Inescapable Loop

Ten years after graduation, ten years of working. Not counting internships, I have worked for 4 companies. I spent 4 years at Tencent - my longest job. But I found that no matter where I went, I eventually became bored and unhappy. It was an endless loop: **new job - bored - new job - bored - ...** with no break condition to escape. I was lost for a long time.

![How to make money without a job?](/content/axiaoxin-2024-summary/how-to-make-money.png)

At both of my Shenzhen jobs, I walked to work. For a while, whether going to or coming from work, I would experience the same scenes that left me in a daze. Same time, same place, the same unfamiliar but familiar face walking past me. Same time, same place, cars with hazy red taillights lining up at traffic lights - the bustling, hurried scene of a big city. **These similar scenes repeated every single day, making me feel like life was boring, and the future would probably just be more of the same. A short life, filled mostly with monotonous repetitive days - I could not accept it. The thought of working like a zombie for another 30 years filled me with despair. This was not the life I wanted.** Add in meaningless work tasks that I had no choice but to do, and I felt nothing but irritability.

Your position always determines your perspective. The reason I chose to be a cog in the machine was precisely because I did not want to build rockets. A cog just wants to be a good cog. If the work you do is only to help others complete their jobs, and the meaning of the work itself no longer matters - then the work is meaningless. Every day, I was cursing inside, no longer happy, trapped in that loop.

I think the reason I fell into this loop was that I was pursuing meaning too obsessively. **A job is just a means of making a living - there is no need to excessively pursue meaning in it.** But I also deeply felt the nausea of certain types of work: the boss's "brilliant ideas," the blurring of work-life boundaries, etc. As an employee, you are dependent on the company. You do not have a direct way to transact with the outside world. I wanted to change but never had the courage to take action - and did not know what action to take. All I could think of was changing jobs. Enter the loop. Trapped in the loop.

### Turning a Hobby into a Side Hustle

As I mentioned earlier, I have always loved programming and writing code, creating things that might be useless. From high school to now, I have stayed the same. I am not particularly prolific, and I do not have any projects worth bragging about, but I simply enjoy doing it. It is a personal hobby - I want to create things that others can use, that give me a sense of accomplishment. In university, I thought programmers could create worlds with their hands - a naive, "one-click opens the gates of heaven" illusion.

![GitHub commit history 2012-2024](/content/axiaoxin-2024-summary/github-commits.png)

My very first side hustle was teaching Python to a working professional outside of university during my college days. If I remember correctly, it was 200 RMB per session. I never had any business mindset, never thought about earning money independently - I just wanted to be an employee. Little did I know back then that even employees can one day find themselves unwanted.

Luckily, by serendipity, I spent my spare time - the time others use for coffee - writing code and building small web pages. I made web pages because besides developing exe programs, that is all I knew. For more people to use my software, I had to make it accessible through a website. Initially, I never thought about making money from it, let alone believed it could make money. I just casually slapped Google AdSense on it. And that casual decision laid a modest foundation for my eventual transition to freelancing. In recent years, traffic gradually came. Google AdSense brought me a 24/7 passive income stream - my websites earn money even while I sleep. No effort is ever wasted, and no success is accidental. All those serendipitous moments are really just the natural result of accumulated effort.

For readers unfamiliar with Google AdSense, let me briefly explain: Google AdSense shows advertisements on web pages you browse. If someone clicks or views an ad, the website author earns some revenue. It is similar to ads inside WeChat Official Account articles - the same principle. Typically, each click or view does not pay much - it is usually measured in "thousands," a few dollars per thousand impressions.

I first heard about Google AdSense in university from my dorm neighbor "Brother Hu." When I was building my blog system with Python, I heard people could make money by putting ads on their blogs. I just listened and did not think much of it. I do not even remember when I first integrated Google AdSense into my site. Back then, I did not even know how to add ad units. I mindlessly pasted some mysterious JavaScript code into my web pages and forgot about it. I did not take it seriously and never thought about making money.

My first website was probably my self-built blog system, deployed on my school lab's server, accessed by IP address. Later, I switched to Sina SAE platform using a Sina domain. For a long time, I could not bear to spend money on my own domain name. I did not pay attention to website operations or SEO. It was not until August 31, 2014, that I bought my first domain and deployed my site on my own VPS. So I estimate I integrated Google AdSense around 2015. I cannot find the exact date I activated AdSense - the oldest data I can see now is from three years ago (December 2021).

![Google AdSense revenue on December 19, 2021](/content/axiaoxin-2024-summary/adsense-2021-12.png)

### Interest-Driven Side Hustle Attempts

**It is only when you get enough positive feedback from doing something that you start taking it seriously.** Fast forward to April 2022. My Google AdSense revenue hit 275 USD in a single day. At an exchange rate of 6.6, that is about 1,800 RMB. A thousand-eight a day - I was laughing all the way. I got excited. If I could maintain this every day, it would be like owning a rental property that pays out online. So I started taking this seriously. I built more websites, systematically studied SEO techniques, and began treating this as my side hustle. **From that point on, I turned my hobby into a side hustle that generates passive income. I felt like I had found a new direction in life.**

![Google AdSense revenue on April 2, 2022](/content/axiaoxin-2024-summary/adsense-2022-04.png)

This experience was a lot like my investing journey. Until I make a certain level of money at something, I do not take it seriously - it is just a game. Only when the income reaches a certain level do I start paying attention and learning more. But investing is not as simple as building websites - I have lost quite a bit in recent years too : ) Anyway, I believe the simplest truths are the most profound. Even something as seemingly outdated as putting ads on web pages - in an era that has moved through mobile internet and into AI - can still generate income using this ancient method. It is not easy. I hope I can sustain it, but I also need to actively embrace the changes of our time. That is the pressure.

I should mention that WeChat Official Accounts also support article ads. The requirement is 500 followers. Once you qualify, you can add ads when writing articles. **For friends who cannot build websites but want 24/7 passive ad income, WeChat Official Account is undoubtedly the easiest way to start.** Of course, there are many similar platforms in China where you can make money - Baidu's Baijiahao, ByteDance's Toutiao, etc. - all allow you to earn ad revenue by publishing articles. But whether it is a website or these platform accounts, the most important thing is traffic. You need enough readers to see meaningful returns. It requires patience and skill.

Many factors affect ad revenue, not just traffic - including ad unit pricing, the match between ads and content, visitor interest alignment with ads, the probability of successful ad display, and the volume of advertiser campaigns. So besides execution, luck plays a huge role in whether this works out.

But the good times did not last. There was a period afterward where I was only making 3 USD a day - not even enough for a bowl of pork knuckle rice under an office building in Shenzhen.

![Google AdSense revenue on October 16, 2022](/content/axiaoxin-2024-summary/adsense-2022-10.png)

### The Dawn of Passive Income

It was precisely because of these drastic income fluctuations that I did not dare slack off. Not every website I built gets traffic, and not every site with traffic keeps it forever. If a site can earn 1 RMB a day, I would build a thousand of them, ten thousand. I figured quantity could eventually lead to quality - play enough games and you will eventually rank up. So whenever I had free time, I kept writing and building. As an independent solo developer - not even comparable to a cottage workshop - I had no plans to create well-known software or acquire millions of users. I just wanted to nibble on the long tail of traffic. That was enough.

Throughout 2022, almost all my spare time outside of work was spent coding. I stayed up late every night, pushing through until 2 or 3 AM. Occasionally, some websites would be maliciously attacked - there are plenty of ill-intentioned people on the internet.

![DDoS attack alert](/content/axiaoxin-2024-summary/ddos-attack.png)

During this period, monthly ad revenue was as low as 1,500 RMB in bad months and as high as over 20,000 in good ones. The violent fluctuations continued. But it gave me a glimmer of hope that I did not have to kill myself at a desk job. It also made my direction clearer. Still, I worried about losing my job. I was still anxious - the future is unknown. If I were in a small, 18-tier city back home, I would feel this income was enough to truly retire early. But I am in Shenzhen, I have a family. Even though I talk about "lying flat" all the time, I cannot truly do it inside - especially with income this unstable.

**There are many ways to develop a side hustle - start from your interests and strengths.** Whether it is building websites or writing a WeChat Official Account, it is hard to see immediate results. At least, with my abilities, I definitely cannot. **It requires execution, patience, the right methods, and luck.**

### Crisis and Opportunity: Middle-Aged Unemployment That Broke the Loop

After the pandemic ended and restrictions lifted, I started seeing people around me getting laid off one after another. I knew it would be my turn eventually. Especially after the boss who hired me left - I knew I had no one to watch my back. With salary structure changes within the team and business mergers, I knew I was not far from being let go. At that time, my expectations for my next job were either remote work or a significant salary increase. But I did not take any action. I even harbored a bit of wishful thinking, hoping to stay at the company and work steadily. Until early 2024, my superior notified me: **my three-year contract was up and would not be renewed. So my mid-career crisis arrived early at age 33 - I was forced into unemployment.**

I have always been a stubborn person - not particularly talented but with a hint of pride. In the past, if work made me unhappy, I would quit. Back then, the word "unemployment" simply was not in my vocabulary. I thought I was capable. But later I realized changing jobs did not change anything - everywhere was the same. As long as it was tolerable, that was good enough. I did not have that many ideals. After the boss who hired me left, I actually wanted to leave too. But I could not be bothered to go through the hassle - interviews take time and energy to prepare for, and I hate the process. So I kept putting it off. Every day, besides worrying about business alerts at night, I was anxious about layoffs.

![](/content/axiaoxin-2024-summary/laid-off-relieved.png)

**When the layoff axe finally fell, my first reaction was relief. I finally relaxed. My anxiety about being laid off vanished instantly. I felt completely light.** The night I got the notice, I thought for a long time about what to do next. Should I look for another job, or try not working and go full-time freelance as an indie developer? I could not figure it out that night - mostly I was worried about the unknown. I simply jotted down a daily schedule in my phone's notes app, planning what my routine would look like if I did not work and went full-time freelance. Deep down, I craved not having a job, but I did not dare. I was afraid. I did not know if my life would fall apart without a steady salary.

### The Forced Path to Freelancing

Faced with sudden yet long-anticipated unemployment, after careful consideration, I wrote a blog post titled ["My Thoughts on Freelancing and Indie Development"](https://blog.axiaoxin.com/post/indiehacker-means/). As I wrote, I sorted out my thoughts. Eventually, I made the decision: **I am done with the office life!** I decided to break the endless cycle of getting a job, getting bored, getting another job. I would temporarily stop looking for work. The job market was terrible anyway - I would spare myself the competition. I would rely on myself, double down on my existing side hustle income, stabilize it, and turn it into a real means of supporting myself independently. I finally had the courage to break out of the loop. But I could not hide the fact that I did not walk out on my own - I was pushed out.

March 8, 2024, was my last day at my previous company. Before leaving, I took a photo of my desk, hoping it was my last desk in corporate life, hoping I could keep going on this freelancing path. **On March 9, I officially started my full-time indie development journey, stepping onto the completely unknown road of freelancing.** I do not know how far I can go. If I cannot make it work, I will find another job. Do my best and leave the rest to fate.

![2024.03.08 16:52](/content/axiaoxin-2024-summary/last-workday.png)

And so, from March until today, I have been waking up naturally every day, no longer forced to get up early and commute. I am living the life I once "dreamed of" while working. Although my income is much lower than before, I am at least supporting myself through various side hustles - and it is passive income.

## My 2024 Overview and Freelancing Challenges

2024 was my first year as a full-time indie developer - a year of deep experience with growth and challenges on the freelancing path. There was the anxiety of effort, and unexpected accomplishments. Every decision, every adjustment, was about finding the most suitable way to survive. Even without clear answers, I kept making earnest attempts.

### January

The business project merger at the company was almost complete. I could sense layoffs were imminent. I bought a 27-inch external monitor for coding at home - I had been using my 15-inch MacBook Pro, and opening multiple windows was a pain. I also bought a small keyboard for my home desk.

Sure enough, on the last day of the month, I was notified that my contract was expiring and I did not need to come to work anymore.

In my spare time, I kept optimizing my websites and removed a lot of ad units - my ad placement was really out of control. I also promoted some high-data-volume phone card plans, but barely made any sales all year. I got into Douyin's mid-video partner program, earning a few cents a day steadily. But recently, the official program was discontinued, and I am not eligible for the new partner program, so that income stream was cut off. I stopped updating videos - they were all AI-generated low-quality content anyway.

I briefly enjoyed life - watched the movie "The Annual Conference Stops" at the cinema downstairs. Remember, in the workplace, always align on granularity with your leaders.

### February

Chinese New Year. Went back to Sichuan for Spring Festival, then headed to Harbin for 5 days. Experienced winter in Northeast China - ice and snow everywhere. Great experience. Northeastern grilled skewers are amazing, and the kvass was good too. Saw heavy snow in Harbin.

![Songhua River](/content/axiaoxin-2024-summary/songhua-river.png)

Had a great time skiing in Harbin. I am not very good - cannot even get out of the beginner zone - but I hit the intermediate slopes at both Yabuli and the New Sports Committee resort. So much fun. Problem is, I do not exercise regularly, so my legs give out after a while. Moral of the story: exercise more, or you will not have the stamina to enjoy yourself when you want to play.

From Harbin, I took a sleeper train to Beijing and spent nearly 3 days there. Got lucky - it snowed heavily in Beijing too.

Back in Shenzhen, I thought about future plans and wrote a blog post: ["I, 33, Am Unemployed"](https://blog.axiaoxin.com/post/jobless/)

### March

March is the month to remember Hai Zi:

> From tomorrow onward, be a freelancer, feed horses, chop wood, travel the world.

Went through the resignation process. Last day - said goodbye to colleagues I got along with. From then on, I became a free but lonely full-time indie developer.

I initially set myself a lot of development and learning tasks, plus various self-media tasks and some CPS affiliate promotion. I wanted to go all-in on all my previous side hustles to increase my income for the days ahead. Now, my side hustle had become my main hustle.

Related reading: [Middle-aged Programmer Laid Off, Forced to Start Plan B - Day 0 as a Freelancer/Indie Developer](https://blog.axiaoxin.com/post/planb-day0/)

Before this, I did not really have a concept of "indie development." I only learned from browsing Twitter that there was a group of people like me who called themselves indie developers. At first, I wanted to use the label too. But the more I saw, the more I felt this label would become like "influencer" - maybe a slightly controversial term in the future. Anyone who truly loves programming, whether formally trained or not, is probably doing what could be called "indie development" in their daily lives. So now I mainly call myself a freelancer. After all, I am not just making money through development - I have a variety of other channels. But they are all small-time stuff. The income from other channels is relatively low, not enough to support living - just pocket money.

After losing the stable cash flow from my salary, I paused some of my regular investment plans to alleviate my insecurity. Losing that stable income messed up my original investment plans. This year's investments were a disaster. The fundamental changes over the past three years have left me directionless - by my own standards, I cannot find a single asset worth holding long-term. But it also made me realize what I really want. I want simple, stable passive income - not a series of laborious financial statement analyses. With that in mind, dividend ETFs are my best choice.

March was also when my commercial insurance premium was deducted. I regret it a bit now. I bought critical illness insurance and term life insurance in 2020, when I was just 30. I chose relatively expensive tiers for both. At the time, I did not consider the possibility of unemployment. Now I regret buying such expensive plans. My total monthly commercial insurance premiums come to about 1,600 RMB. I used to think that amount was trivial - only when every cent has to be earned through your own effort do you realize how hard that 1,600 is.

Successfully transferred my Shanghai social security contributions to Shenzhen. Also successfully applied for unemployment benefits - they would pay out until December, a bit over 2,000 RMB per month. I agonized for a long time over whether to continue paying social security myself. After weighing the pros and cons based on my situation, I decided to continue. During the unemployment benefit period, the government covers medical insurance. I only need to pay for personal pension insurance - less than 800 RMB per month. Once the unemployment benefits run out, I will have to pay for everything myself. There is pressure everywhere in life.

In March, I visited Jieyang for 2 days. Completed the development workflow for a mini-program and launched a page with no real functionality. Ended up abandoning it for various reasons - paused my mini-program learning and development.

The first month as a full-time indie developer came with many adjustments, especially having to cook lunch for myself. March's income was satisfying - over 20,000 RMB - giving me the necessary confidence to stick with this path.

### April

April - received my severance package. The money was in the bank. No more salary to collect. One word: liberating. Let me enjoy this temporary relief.

This month was a blur of self-media articles, short videos, and coding - busy as hell. Got my video account to 1,000 followers. Whether it is short videos or WeChat Official Account, monetization is hard. WeChat accounts are slightly better.

My blog's update frequency increased dramatically after being laid off. My blog had very little ad income before, but after various ups and downs, it is starting to pick up - averaging enough for a bottle of cola a day.

I also worked on SEO backlinks everywhere. Result: my Douban account got banned. I was updating Mowen Notes too - paid subscriptions were not working well. My WeChat Official Account articles also got deleted. My Baijiahao account got banned this month too. It is so hard. **If you want to do self-media, you must first understand the rules of each platform.**

SEO is something that requires continuous accumulation. Even though I think SEO may not work even if you do it - a lot of it depends on luck. There are many SEO courses online; I have not watched any. I think since SEO is about optimizing for search engines, you should go to the official search engine documentation and learn from there. I admit quality courses can save learners time and help them avoid detours, but I still recommend self-learning. It is a test of execution - otherwise, even after buying the course, it is hard to gain much. **The best SEO strategy is to forget about SEO and focus on providing valuable content to users.** I deeply feel that when you provide content users need, they will spontaneously share your link on social media or the internet - creating both virality and backlinks. ## Experience and Lessons: Reflection and Growth

Before publishing this article, I read through it several times. Although the main purpose was to write for myself, I also wondered: would other readers find inspiration? ### Personal Growth and Learning

- Interest is the best teacher. Self-directed learning and practice are crucial. Work should not just be about survival - it should also fulfill personal interests and growth. Maintaining a balance between hobbies and work not only improves quality of life but can also bring new career opportunities. Combine learning with practice - input must be accompanied by output to make yourself more competitive.
- Maintain an independent personality and think independently. Put yourself in others' shoes. Ask yourself more: what? why? what makes you think so? What if you were them? What if they were you? Be patient - patient in action, patient in speaking. Whatever you do, big or small, take it seriously and do it to the best of your ability.
- Still grateful for AI, which makes everyone an "expert" in their field. Of course, AI's limitations are obvious. AI is an amplifier of ability, not a silver bullet.
- When you deliberately work at something, you will definitely gain from it. You can deliberately practice anything, including making money. How much you gain depends on your methods, personal resources, the ceiling of the task, and - luck. When luck comes, you need the ability to catch it.
- Ignore non-practitioner commentary. Ignore comments from people without practical experience. Those making them may never have been through it, and even if they have, they may not have deep insight. Everyone's situation is different. Learn to identify whether feedback has real value. Once you have identified your direction, try it - do not let others' opinions affect your mindset.

### Work and Career Development

- Do not be overly anxious about career choices. Opportunities often come from your courage to try and explore. Sometimes, unexpected opportunities can bring unexpected changes. Although career and achievement matter, the meaning of life is not just about work and money. Family and love are equally precious parts of life. Keep an open and positive mindset, and give yourself the chance to experience the multiple beauties of life.
- If you keep falling into the "new job - bored - new job - bored" cycle, the problem may not be your job choices, but your attitude and expectations toward work. The excessive pursuit of meaning in work often leads to anxiety and dissatisfaction. The meaning of work does not need to be amplified. A job is just a means of making a living - do not over-pursue meaning in the work itself. In reality, most people work not for meaning, but for livelihood and stability.
- Do not rely solely on your job. Times change. No job is permanent. Treat every job as temporary. Always keep learning - that is the real answer to facing the future. Ask yourself: what can I provide, not what do others want me to do. After all, how long you can work is ultimately someone else's decision. Job security is always created by ourselves, not granted by the boss.

### Side Hustle Development

- Starting from your interests, finding your side hustle and achieving passive income through it is a viable direction. Without a clear business mindset, I coded and built small tools out of interest, eventually earning through Google AdSense - proving that interests and side hustles can be combined. To succeed in side hustles, you must persist. Do not give up easily because of short-term failures or fluctuations. You need patience, and you need to continuously adjust your strategy along the way to find the right methods.
- Even pure interests and hobbies can gradually develop into a side hustle. The key is whether you consistently invest in it and whether you can get positive feedback from it. Do not rush to pursue profitability early on. As time and effort accumulate, returns will gradually become meaningful. Side hustles like building websites or writing a WeChat Official Account are hard to see returns in the short term - they require patience and execution. My experience tells me that only through sustained investment and continuous attempts can you gradually see returns.
- When engaged in side hustles, do not just focus on the size of the income. Interest and a sense of accomplishment are equally important sources of motivation. Find something you like and find meaningful - it can make your side hustle not just a money-making tool, but a part of life's enjoyment. When your side hustle starts to pay off, do not hesitate - seize the opportunity and invest more energy.
- Internet platforms provide enormous opportunities for personal entrepreneurship and side hustle development. Although these avenues seem simple, they require certain skills and strategies to use effectively. Online platforms are important tools for modern entrepreneurs, but they also require us to master traffic, SEO, and other techniques, and to have a long-term vision.
- Do not look down on every cent and penny - many a little makes a mickle. Being able to generate one cent of income at least verifies that this is a possible way to make money. If you can generate 1 RMB a day, that is equivalent to having 30,000 RMB in bank deposit interest.
- Be cautious with spending and investing. Avoid "bankruptcy-style" collapse.

### The Reality of Freelancing

- Freelancing is not free. It requires more self-discipline. Family understanding and support are very important. Diving headfirst into making money as a full-time indie developer - on the surface, you have a lot of time you can arrange yourself. In reality, you are busier than you ever were at a job. Unemployment does not represent personal failure. On the contrary, it can be an opportunity to re-examine your career goals and explore new possibilities.
- Any side hustle will inevitably experience fluctuations and uncertainty. Stay flexible and keep learning from failures. Maintain a positive attitude when facing lows. Income instability often brings anxiety - it is a challenge most freelancers or side hustlers must face. You have to consider how to balance unstable income with family responsibilities and other practical issues. Never ignore the pressure of income instability. Appropriate risk management is necessary.
- Is the lack of external interaction a problem in indie development? Whether you are employed or an indie developer, the problem is not lack of communication - it is lack of effective communication.
- Network attacks or plagiarism are inevitable. You must learn how to defend against them, especially when your income depends on websites or online platforms.

### Mindset Management

- Life is not a straight line. When encountering setbacks and confusion, do not be overly anxious or stubborn. Adjust your direction timely and try new opportunities - sometimes it may lead you to discover more possibilities. Face life's imperfections and impermanence rationally. Being pessimistic about life does not mean being negative - it means clearly seeing reality's shortcomings and difficulties. Accepting life's impermanence can bring more inner peace. Fate may be beyond our control, but we can choose how to face and respond to it.
- Reduce anxiety. The root cause of anxiety is wanting to do many things at once and expecting immediate results. Focus on one thing. Let go of the expectation to see results immediately. This can effectively alleviate anxiety.
- When you have an idea, just go do it. This path may not work out smoothly, but along the way, you might discover other paths. If you do not do it, you will never succeed - it is all just a dream. Be mentally prepared for failure.

### Practical Experience

- Do what you are best at in the way others need it most - you will definitely gain something. Use your strengths to create value for others. Do not constantly talk about "being altruistic." As long as a person is still distinguishing between "self" and "other," it is not true altruism.
- You must have output. Many people have a lot of input but never produce anything. Try creating output. If you cannot write code, write articles. Write publicly. Writing also helps you think.
- Whether in full-time work or side hustles, you will face career instability - especially freelancing and entrepreneurial work. When dealing with income instability, you need coping strategies - like maintaining a balance between your main job and side hustle, or maintaining a financial reserve before your side hustle income stabilizes.
- Value your personal brand building. Accumulate followers. Without traffic, even if you have abundant resources, they cannot be turned into income.
- If you can make money from something, it is because you have done it well enough to earn money. Whether it is self-media or programming, only if you truly love it and operate it with heart will you gain something. If you start a self-media account or build software purely for money, you will often fail to get positive feedback quickly, become disappointed, and eventually give up - then go around proclaiming this path is hopeless.

## 2024 Selected Book List

Books I read this year that I feel are worth rereading:

**1. *The Almanack of Naval Ravikant***

Getting rich is not luck, and happiness does not fall from the sky. Accumulating wealth and living a happy life are skills we can learn. This book collects and organizes the life wisdom shared by Silicon Valley investor Naval Ravikant over the past decade through Twitter, podcasts, and interviews. It shares Naval's principles and methods for wealth accumulation and a happy life. Naval not only tells readers how to get rich - he also tells them how to view life and how to find happiness. His thinking on wealth and life will help you walk your unique path toward a richer, happier life.

Many of the ideas in this book resonated deeply with my own experiences and feelings - things I have thought but could not articulate. When you see these relatable thoughts expressed by someone else, it is invigorating. It gives you confidence because you share similarities with a successful person.

**2. *Educating Children Through Reading***

The author provides excellent methods - using reading to inspire and educate children. This perfectly matches my ideal approach to education. The book clearly describes what books children at different stages with different problems should read, and what feedback and guidance parents should provide. Reading educates not just children, but also yourself. Although I am not yet a parent, if my freelancing path goes smoothly, I will inevitably become a so-called "full-time dad" or "full-time husband" in the future. I also want to "re-raise" myself. While accompanying my children's growth as a parent, I also want to diligently cultivate myself - to be a good "guide" for both myself and my children.

**3. *Cognitive Awakening: The Driving Force for Self-Change***

This book also expresses many viewpoints that I share but could not put into words. In particular, it summarizes the essence of anxiety in one sentence. I had identified the causes of my own anxiety through exploration, but I could not articulate it as sharply. That is the cognitive gap. But on the other hand, I wonder: if I had read the author's summary without having experienced it myself, I would not have felt such deep resonance.

The root cause of anxiety: "Wanting to do many things at once while expecting immediate results. All human suffering is essentially anger at one's own powerlessness. Your desires exceed your abilities, and you are extremely lacking in patience. Anxiety arises from the gap between desire and ability."

**Methods to resolve anxiety**:

- Curb your desires - do not try to do many things at once
- Face reality - clearly see your true ability level
- Prioritize important tasks - find ways to only do the most important things
- Accept your environment - do what you can within limitations
- Confront the core - push yourself hard to break through

I read fewer books this year. The quantity can be low, but after finishing a book, you must change yourself in some way - internalize it and make it something you can use. Even if it is just one small point, it is growth, and it is a gain. Learn and apply. Use output to force input - it works very well.

> After reading a book, go practice the principles in it. Even if just one or two points change your life, that is remarkable - because from that moment on, the knowledge in the book has been transformed. When you can clearly explain newly learned knowledge, it means you have incorporated it into your own knowledge system, reached the level of teaching others, and may even create new knowledge. The highest level of teaching is explaining what you are teaching in the simplest terms so a layperson can understand.
>
> Forcing yourself to obtain high-quality knowledge, deeply connect new knowledge, and then teach others in your own words or writing - that is a path of deep learning. Such learning inevitably means giving up the security of learning fast and learning a lot. It takes more time, faces more difficulty, and can even be "agonizing."
>
> As much as possible, obtain and personally study first-hand knowledge. Write what you have learned in your own words. Reflect on life - learning is not just about reading books. Life experiences can also be deeply learned from.
>
> Knowledge only becomes yours when it helps you make practical decisions. True knowledge is not knowing something - it is being able to use it to make correct judgments and choices and solve practical problems. The purpose of personal growth is not "knowing and understanding" - it is "judging and choosing."
>
> So-called learning and growth - things like reading, writing, reflecting, building habits, practicing skills, establishing cognitive systems - are essentially the same thing: expanding outward bit by bit at the edge of your comfort zone. Pay attention to the one thing that touches you most each day. Whether it is a good insight or a bad experience, write it down and review it. The more detailed, the better.
>
> Produce something - either speak, write, or share videos... In short, do not just keep learning endlessly without any output. Because learning without feedback is not only painful but will almost certainly fail.
>
> Teaching is the best way to learn; using is the best way to learn; output drives input; let your work speak for itself. Without works and a works mindset, nothing else matters. If you have carefully crafted your work, someone will definitely think you are better than them. At that point, praise will come to you. And people who are truly better than you usually have no time to criticize you, so you do not need to worry about being mocked.
