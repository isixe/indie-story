Building in Public is a popular practice in the indie dev community. Here's my experience and what I got out of it.

## What Is Building in Public

Simply put, it's sharing your product development process openly on social media:
- Product ideas and decisions
- Progress and milestones
- Revenue data (optional)
- Successes and failures

## My Approach

I started sharing daily updates on Twitter:
- Morning: today's plan
- Evening: what I accomplished
- Weekly summaries
- Monthly income reports

## The Benefits

1. **Accountability**: Public commitments keep you moving
2. **Early feedback**: Get user input before launch
3. **Build an audience**: Accumulate followers and potential users
4. **Marketing channel**: Seed users ready at launch

## How to Start

- Pick a platform (Twitter, Xiaohongshu, Jike)
- Set a consistent posting cadence
- Share authentic, valuable content
- Engage with the community, don't just broadcast

Building in public isn't for everyone, but if you want to build a personal brand, it's a great way to start.
