A year ago, I quit my big-tech job and started my indie development journey. It's been a year of wins and setbacks, and I want to document what I've learned.

## Why Go Indie

After five years in big tech, the pay was good but something was missing. Every day felt the same — watching product decisions get diluted by layers of approval, slowly losing the passion for creation.

## First Product

My first product was a simple Pomodoro timer app. Two months to build, fewer than 100 downloads in the first month. But I didn't give up — I iterated on user feedback, and now it has over 50,000 users.

## Revenue

Total first-year revenue was around ¥150k, mainly from in-app purchases and ads. Much less than my salary, but the satisfaction of doing what I love? Priceless.

## Advice for Beginners

1. Don't quit your job — validate your idea on the side first
2. Focus on one product, don't spread too thin
3. Listen to user feedback, iterate fast
4. Be patient — success takes time

Indie development isn't easy, but if you love it, it's worth the fight.
