Behind every successful project are countless failures. Today I want to share three of mine and what they taught me.

## Project 1: Social App

A social platform for programmers. Great idea, but it failed.

**Why it failed**:
- Social products need critical mass for network effects
- Cold start was brutal with no acquisition channels
- Too many competitors, not enough differentiation

**Lesson**: Don't build social products unless you have serious resources.

## Project 2: AI Writing Tool

I rushed out an AI writing tool when ChatGPT blew up.

**Why it failed**:
- Low technical barrier, flooded market
- No unique value proposition
- Terrible user retention

**Lesson**: Chasing hype without a moat is a losing strategy.

## Project 3: Vertical SaaS

A niche tool for a specific industry, six months in development.

**Why it failed**:
- Never deeply understood the real user needs
- Market too small, ceiling too low
- Sales cycles were painfully long

**Lesson**: B2B requires deep industry knowledge — small markets are hard to scale.

Failure isn't fatal if you learn from it. Hope my scars help you avoid the same mistakes.
