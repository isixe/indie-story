> 2024-04-08 "My Story with LeaferJS"

## Three Years Through the Darkness

Spring has come again with its sunny days. The willows by the river have sprouted tender green shoots, and everything is vying to revive.

![Sprouting](/images/leaferjs-indie-dev-story/spring.jpg)

Come to think of it, it's been exactly three years since I started my entrepreneurial journey in Beijing. I can hardly believe I've endured this long.

Through those three long, dark years — facing uncertainty, facing depleted funds — I can't imagine how I managed to persist.

Can you imagine waking up every day doing what you love, your heart full of hope?

Can you imagine being three years into your venture, drowning in debt, yet still pushing forward?

Can you imagine that in another month, I might have to sell my house just to keep my startup alive? [Someone has since stepped in to help.]

Can you imagine everyone else busy celebrating the New Year while I'm still launching a product?

Countless nights, invisible pressures bore down on me, leaving me anxious and unable to sleep.

I dream of seeing the world beyond — the rapeseed flowers of Wuyuan, the ancient city walls of Xi'an, the Erhai Lake in Dali, the quiet and ancient towns, the colorful forests, the majestic mountains and seas, and tasting diverse cuisines.

The harsh reality is I can't go anywhere [facepalm].

## Doing Something Worthwhile

Many people have asked me why I didn't build a team.

I've thought about building a team. I've tried. But starting a business based on foundational technology is a difficult and long road. Facing uncertain outcomes and immense pressure to make a living, it's hard for anyone who isn't driven by passion to stay the course. So, perhaps it's better for me to take this journey first.

![Climbing](/images/leaferjs-indie-dev-story/mountain.jpg)

People also ask: can one person really build a business? What if you can't secure funding?

I think the real answer lies within us — how badly do we want to make this happen?

Fortunately, I have enough passion for this work. I firmly believe this is a great goal worth dedicating the rest of my life to.

If the direction is right, I believe these things will gradually fall into place along the entrepreneurial journey.

An arrow shot has no return. To realize the blueprint in my heart, I've been like a bamboo shoot yet to break the surface, growing downward in the soil, building a strong foundation.

The difficulty and complexity of this endeavor have far exceeded my imagination. It demands enough patience and creativity to see it through.

## ♫ Bloom in Adversity

As I journeyed deeper into entrepreneurship, I encountered countless unprecedented difficulties and gradually found myself in dire straits. Yet I refused to be crushed by fear. To see the day when the seed would bloom, I never stopped moving forward and gritted my teeth to persevere.

It was this very experience that led me to write a song — "Bloom in Adversity" — to inspire myself to keep going. When I play it, the vision of my dream draws closer, the fighting spirit reignites, and I find the strength to face the challenges and pressures ahead.

So, what exactly happened over these three years? How did I get through it? How did my inner world change? Let me share slowly.

## Embarking on the Entrepreneurial Journey

The story begins three years ago. In early 2021, driven by a thirst for the unknown and to prepare for the infamous "age 35 crisis" in tech, I courageously embarked on my entrepreneurial journey.

![Seed](/images/leaferjs-indie-dev-story/seed.jpg)

After two months of intensive development, I had initially validated the feasibility of building a **graphics engine capable of running millions of elements**.

If this vision became reality, the websites, documents, and software we use could carry ten times more content than before, and pages would open and browse faster than ever.

I didn't hesitate to dive in, hoping to turn this seemingly impossible task into reality.

Back then, I was brimming with passion and energy, never feeling tired.

In the night, the air was filled with the scent of code and music. After long hours of work, I would play the piano, then dive back in.

## First Fruits

Four months later, bursting with joy, I brought the first version of the technology to friends and users, drawing looks of amazement. Everyone was astonished by how smoothly 1 million elements ran on a single canvas. This also brought aboard my first collaborator.

As we all know, foundational technology is hard to monetize. So we decided to pick a commercially mature product in the market as our collaborative project, using it as a vehicle to put the technology to use.

However, the product's development difficulty far exceeded expectations. After six grueling months of pressure and hard work, we finally released the first beta.

Powered by this technology, the product caused an immediate sensation upon launch. Investors came knocking, including some top-tier domestic investment firms. Those were the most glorious days since we started our venture.

## The Partnership Ends

But because we weren't a team with a strong pedigree, investors held back, hoping to see further product development. However, fully maturing this product into a production-ready version would still take time — time we could hardly afford.

In the end, regrettably, it happened. Burdened by financial pressure and uncertainty about the future, my partner and I were forced to stop the product collaboration.

## Lost in Thought

This hit us pretty hard. I spent months lying low. With little savings left, I was torn between continuing to develop the product (which meant rebuilding a team) or returning to my original technical path (something I could do alone). I was anxious and hesitant, caught in a long period of deep reflection. I began to search my own heart…

As the pandemic receded, I realized how fragile life is. In this short and precious lifetime, I should try to do things no one has done before — things that bring value to the world.

I thought, I need a reason like this: even if I fail, it will have been worth it.

This experience also taught me a profound lesson: without passion and a sense of purpose, it's incredibly hard for anyone to persevere through hardship.

So I made a pivotal decision: pause the commercial product, settle in to focus on LeaferJS, and open-source it for more people to use.

Because I know deep down that LeaferJS is the thing that will change the world.

## Starting Anew

During those difficult times, music became one of the ways I conversed with the darkness. I wrote my first song. I believed that if I persisted, after the storm, I'd eventually see a beautiful rainbow.

Building a general-purpose graphics rendering engine meant there was still enormous work ahead. Over the next long 10 months (I honestly didn't expect it would take this long), I devoted myself entirely to development.

In November 2022 — I remember it clearly — I had spent all my savings. After that, the support of my family and friends, along with my passion for this undertaking, kept me going.

I rewrote LeaferJS to the highest standards, added a modern UI drawing framework, and wrote friendly documentation with code examples.

![Site Cover](/images/leaferjs-indie-dev-story/leaferjs.jpg)

## The First Light of Dawn

On June 28, 2023, I officially open-sourced LeaferJS. The announcement sparked hugeresponse across major platforms, earning recommendations from OSChina (Open Source China), Zhihu, the Tech Enthusiast Weekly newsletter, and others. Notably, after an influential user published a deep-dive article analyzing LeaferJS's performance, it was widely reposted by frontend-focused accounts. Thousands of users visited our website every day; the user group filled up instantly. Looking at the numbers, my excitement was beyond words. I knew this decision was right.

After all these years, I finally saw a faint glimmer of dawn. It was the light I had dreamed of. Though I knew there was still a long road ahead before this technology sees widespread adoption, it undoubtedly gave me the courage to keep going. This was a new beginning, and also a more formidable challenge.

## Enterprises Begin to Adopt

As LeaferJS's user base grew, many people joined in. They not only actively used LeaferJS but also brought forward practical needs and valuable suggestions.

Over the next 9 months, I continued polishing and upgrading LeaferJS. Despite many challenges, I completed cross-platform development capabilities and important features like a graphical editor.

The technology and documentation kept improving — not just through my own efforts, but through the participation of every user.

Watching company after company start using my technology to launch their products filled me with immense relief and pride.

They use LeaferJS for all kinds of scenarios: drawing mini-program posters, developing mini-games, big-data tables, poster editors, image editors, video editors, power monitoring systems, architectural drawing software, engraving software, painting software, design software, and more.

In the near future, LeaferJS might even be used for game and application development, along with countless other scenarios.

![Open Source](/images/leaferjs-indie-dev-story/open.png)

These are some of the open-source products and articles featured on our official site. Many commercial products are not listed here for confidentiality.

## Selected as OSChina's 2023 Major Announcement

At the close of 2023, LeaferJS received its first honor — being selected as one of OSChina's (Open Source China) major announcements of the year, marking recognition from the domestic open-source community.

![Major Announcement](/images/leaferjs-indie-dev-story/award-2023.jpg)

## Accelerating Performance

Over the past three years, I've laid a solid foundation for this graphics engine, achieving excellent results in element creation speed and memory efficiency, and creating value for many companies.

This year, I plan to dramatically improve interaction performance. This requires meticulous optimization across many areas — a significant challenge.

To give everyone a taste of the upcoming performance, I built a [canvas that holds one million cups of coffee](https://www.leaferjs.com/coffee/), with smooth interaction. Feel free to try it out. There are still many rough edges, and I'll keep optimizing.

![One Million Cups of Coffee Canvas](/images/leaferjs-indie-dev-story/cafe.jpg)

Our ultimate goal is to achieve smooth interaction with **millions of static elements** and **hundreds of thousands of dynamic game elements** (accelerated by WebGPU).

## Building Parts and Products Like Constructing the Real World

Once the features and performance reach a certain stage, LeaferJS can be applied much more broadly.

It can not only be used for efficient drawing, configuration, and building graphics/image editing software — in the future, it could also power WYSIWYG web pages, applications, games, and animations. As the feature set and ecosystem mature, integration with AI will bring wave after wave of entrepreneurial opportunities.

Imagine: in the future, we can build things like constructing the real world — dividing labor in areas where each of us excels, producing parts and products in a WYSIWYG manner, and circulating them commercially. This could dramatically boost productivity and unleash individual creativity!

To achieve this, we need to establish a mutually recognized open standard. Free and open-source LeaferJS can serve as that bridge.

## We Need Help and Participation from the Community

We've always been committed to advancing technology. Yet the power of an individual is always limited, especially when facing shortages of funding, promotion, team, tutorials, examples, and plugins.

So I sincerely invite you to become a participant in this great dream! Let's build an open-source community that belongs to all of us!

Whether you're an experienced developer, someone passionate about design, or someone who wants to support us as a sponsor — you will play a vital role in this journey.

If you have technical expertise, consider developing plugins, examples, or products. Many users are eager to benefit from your skills.

If you're good at communication, consider providing tutorials or spreading our story. Many fans want to follow you.

If you're multilingual, consider contributing translations so people around the world can enjoy the benefits LeaferJS brings.

As a supporter, a simple Star from you can amplify LeaferJS's impact.

And as a sponsor, a warm cup of coffee from you will provide us meaningful support.

![coffee](/images/leaferjs-indie-dev-story/coffee.svg)

Love begets love, blessings beget blessings. I believe your contributions will be repaid many times over, creating a butterfly effect that brings LeaferJS to more people.

## Building a Remarkable Open-Source Company

For LeaferJS to grow further, it needs a new entity. I plan to found a brand-new open-source company for LeaferJS at the right time this year.

I'll seek outstanding participants from the community who share this passion, form an initial team, and jointly build a high-performance 2D/3D graphics engine for professional-grade product development in the AI era. We'll also drive ecosystem growth, set up a developer store, and enable productivity to flow.

Every early participant — whether a media voice, community contributor, sponsor, or Stargazer — will be recorded and gathered onto a wall of faces visible to everyone, as a testament to our shared journey.

To focus on developing the graphics engine and avoid competing with our customers, I plan to prioritize selling the previously developed Wodisign product before operating it myself. I believe that with our support, it will become a highly competitive product.

## The End of the Story

Three years — both long and short. Some people spent three years building a highly-anticipated car. Some spent three years making a blockbuster film. And I spent these three years experiencing a blazing rebirth from the depths of adversity — a feeling only I can truly understand.

The story ends on a relatively positive note. Now I have LeaferJS by my side. I'm about to emerge from the predicament of life. The fields of rapeseed flowers are waving at me, as if telling me that spring has arrived. I strum the notes in my heart and passionately play "Bloom in Adversity."
