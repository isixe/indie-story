Many indie developers are great at building products but terrible at marketing. Here's how to get users on a zero budget.

## Content Marketing

### Blogging
- Write articles that solve user problems
- Share your dev process and experience
- Industry analysis and trend predictions

Publish on:
- Personal blog (long-term SEO value)
- Zhihu, Jianshu (China traffic)
- Medium, Dev.to (international traffic)

### Video Content
- Product demos
- Tutorials and guides
- Dev vlogs

Platforms:
- Bilibili (China devs)
- YouTube (international)
- Douyin/Xiaohongshu (mass audience)

## Community Marketing

### Vertical Communities
- V2EX, Juejin (developers)
- Jike (product managers)
- Reddit, Indie Hackers (international)

**Tips**:
- Provide value first, promote later
- Join discussions, build reputation
- Share real experience, no spam

### Q&A Platforms
- Zhihu: answer relevant questions
- Quora: English Q&A
- Stack Overflow: technical questions

## Product-Led Growth

### Freemium Model
- Free basic features
- Paid premium features
- Low friction to try

### Viral Mechanics
- Referral rewards
- Social sharing
- User-generated content

### SEO
- Solve what users search for
- Create tool-type pages
- Earn organic traffic

## Partnership Marketing

- Partner with complementary products
- Cross-promotions
- Co-host events

## My Marketing Priorities

1. Content marketing (blog, social)
2. Community engagement (vertical communities)
3. Product optimization (word of mouth)
4. SEO (long-term traffic)

Marketing isn't a one-time task. Find where your users hang out and deliver value there consistently.
